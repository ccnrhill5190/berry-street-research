Open-Ended Response Themes

Grouped from 151 free-text responses across the open-ended questions.

1. Session Continuity Most frequent

Providers described their single biggest workflow need as quickly answering "Where did we leave off?" before the patient joins. The last session recap is not optional — it's the foundation everything else builds on.

"I need to know what we talked about last time before anything else. If I can't find that quickly I'm already behind."

2. Follow-Up Message as Ground Truth

Many providers treat the post-session follow-up message they sent to the patient as their de facto session recap — it's more accurate and faster to read than a chart note. Surfacing this in the prep view is a quick win.

"The message I send after every session has everything in it. I wish I could just see that when I open the profile."

3. Clinical Data Is Hidden

Medications, supplements, allergies, and labs require multiple navigations to find. Providers want a persistent, always-visible clinical snapshot — not buried in Documents or a separate tab.

"I can never remember where the meds list lives. There's no single place that just tells me what they're on."

4. Insurance Urgency

Sessions remaining and insurance expiration need to be proactive alerts — not a stat the provider has to hunt for. Several providers mentioned discovering mid-session that a patient had no coverage left.

"I found out at the start of a session that the patient had used their last covered visit. That should never happen."

5. Journal Data Not Trusted

Macro averages (weekly mood score, overall trends) are dismissed as noisy and context-free. Providers want to see day-level entries so they can understand the story behind the numbers. Many prefer data from external apps.

"An average score means nothing. I need to see the actual days — what happened Tuesday, what happened Friday."

6. Provider-Private Notes

Providers want a "sticky note" per patient that is invisible to the patient, carries forward session to session, and holds quick reminders that don't belong in the official chart. This is a high-desire, easy-win feature.

"I just need a private scratchpad. Something they can't see. Right now I keep a separate doc for this."

7. Goals Need Structure

Short-term session homework and long-term care goals are conflated in the UI, leading to duplication with notes. Providers want two distinct tiers: what we're working on this session vs. the overall care arc.

"Goals and notes overlap too much. I end up just writing the same thing twice in different places."

8. Care Plan Is Buried

The AI-generated care plan from session 1 is valued but becomes impossible to find after a few sessions. Providers want it surfaced in the prep view alongside a session counter.

"There's a care plan somewhere from when we started but I can never find it anymore. It's buried in Documents."

9. Suggested Tasks = Noise for Busy Providers

Suggested tasks received the highest "Never" rate of any card sort item. For high-caseload providers, these feel like clutter rather than help. Opt-in or off-by-default is strongly indicated.

"I don't have time to evaluate suggestions before a session. I need to see what's required, not what's nice to have."

10. Read Receipts & Engagement Signals

Providers want to know if patients opened messages, handouts, and care plan links. Without this, they can't tell whether to follow up or whether the patient is simply non-responsive.

"Did they read what I sent? I have no idea. I end up asking at the start of every session which wastes time."

Design Implications

Ranked by signal strength (frequency × severity) from the card sort and open-ended data.

1. 30-Second Prep Card Highest leverage

A single at-a-glance surface before the session starts: last session recap, goals, journal delta since last session, new messages. The single most-requested pattern across all data sources.

2. Surface Last Follow-Up Message in Prep

Quick win. Show the most recent post-session message the provider sent. This is their de facto recap and takes one query to surface.

3. Persistent Clinical Snapshot

Meds + supplements, allergies, active diagnoses, and most recent labs in one always-visible panel — not buried in Documents or a sub-tab.

4. Provider-Only Notes Field

A private sticky note per patient, invisible to the patient, that carries forward. Easy to build, extremely high desire.

5. Insurance Alert

Proactive banner when sessions remaining drops to a threshold (e.g. ≤3). Must be surfaced before the session — not discovered during it.

6. Tasks: Opt-In, Not Default

Required tasks for some providers, invisible for others. Suggested tasks off by default. The audience is too split for a single default-on configuration.

7. Two-Tier Goals

Separate short-term session homework from long-term care goals. Reduces duplication with notes and gives each a clear home.

8. Day-Level Journal View

Replace or supplement aggregate scores with a timeline of individual entries. Build trust before promoting the journal feature to patients.

9. Care Plan in Prep

Make the AI care plan discoverable from the prep view with a session counter (e.g. "Session 7 of 12"). Stop it from getting lost in Documents.

10. Engagement Signals

Read receipts on messages, handouts, and care plan links. Reduces "did you see what I sent?" from taking up session time.
